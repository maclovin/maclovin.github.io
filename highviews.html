Here's some music reviews by myself. It's not a pro review based in studies about
production. That's my two cents. Each one means something to me and changed my ears.

Miles Davis - Kind of Blue (1959)

 Maybe this sound a little cliche for the most of Jazz specialists, but still being a masterpiece that should be played for all the kids who's starting developing your musicianship. As a son of a guy who owns an incredible Jazz collection I had to bought him the box (CD + DVD + Book) of this amazing work. Listened to this helped me understand how truly express your feelings through elegant melodies and frequencies.

Thee Oh Sees - Floating Coffin (2013)

 The first time I heard this album I don't liked.
Years later I sit down after take a great breakfast, smoke a pot and start work at my desk. Really cool lyrics, kinda melancholic like Putrifiers II, a little louder, fuzzy, with energetic beats. Also a great self-release album produced by Castle Face (Dwyer's label). Perfect to listen on a hard daywork.

Jam City - Classical Curves (2012)

 Do you remember some soundtracks from '80s sci-fi movies? Ok, put the same spirit around a cultural future prediction with a high sound fidellity. That's what Jam City (with Night Slugs) has been working for years. And Classical Curves is one of my favorite records from this amazing collective-label. It's a new way to mix grime, ballroom house, ambient and some future ghetto aesthetics together.
